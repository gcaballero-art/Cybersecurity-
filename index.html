Part 1: Common Types of Cyberattacks

To protect your house, you first need to know if you're protecting against a burglar, a storm, or a fire. It's the same in the digital world.

Phishing

Think of it like fishing with a hook and bait.

A criminal sends an email that looks legitimate‚Äîit might seem to be from a trusted supplier, a bank, or even your own CEO.

  • Contains a 'bait' (link or attachment)
  • Steals passwords and financial information
  • Can install malicious software

It's simple, but incredibly effective.

Ransomware

Imagine a kidnapper who doesn't take a person, but instead takes all your company's files.

This software encrypts everything:

  • Your documents
  • Customer databases
  • Financial records

You then receive a ransom demand, usually in cryptocurrency.

This can completely stop your operations.

DDoS Attack

Distributed Denial of Service

Imagine a small shop with only one door. A DDoS attack is like sending a thousand people to stand in front of that door at the same time.

  • Floods website with traffic
  • Makes services unavailable
  • No legitimate customers can get in

Part 3: Prevention Strategies

These examples are scary, I know. But the good news is that we are not helpless. There are practical, effective steps we can take to protect ourselves.

üõ°Ô∏è Your First Line of Defence

The single most important defence you have is not technology; it's your people.

Building a Human Firewall

  • Regular and engaging employee training
  • Teach staff how to spot phishing emails
  • Run simulations and practice scenarios
  • Create a culture where it's okay to ask: "Is this email safe?"

A well-trained, cautious workforce is your best protection.

üìã

Clear Security Policies

The rules of the road for your company's data.

Example Policy:

Any financial request over ¬£5,000 must be verified through a second communication channel (direct phone call or in-person check).

This simple step would have prevented the AI voice attack we discussed.
